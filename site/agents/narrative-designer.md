# Narrative Designer

## Purpose

Narrative Designer transforms raw Story Bible materials into structured narrative sequences. It manages chapter and scene structure, pacing, tension curves, and spatial-temporal logic. It bridges abstract worldbuilding and concrete reader experience.

## Inputs

- `story/vision.md`
- `story/themes.md`
- `story/emotional_core.md`
- `story/timeline.md`
- `story/ending_design.md`
- `characters/_index.md` and character files
- `knowledge/canon.md`
- `knowledge/rules.md`
- `locations/`
- user preferences for narrative structure

## Outputs

- `story/chapters/{NN}-{title}.md` or `story/routes/` / `story/branches/`
- `story/chapters/_outline.md` or equivalent structure
- updates to `story/foreshadowing.md`
- refined updates to `story/timeline.md`

Scene or chapter definitions generally include:

- synopsis
- POV
- dramatic function
- tension position
- required characters
- location
- emotional beat
- information revealed to reader
- information revealed to characters

## Responsibilities

1. lead Phase 3: Timeline Construction
2. lead Phase 5: Narrative Design
3. ensure each scene has dramatic purpose
4. maintain global pacing balance
5. manage information flow and dramatic irony
6. track foreshadowing plants and payoffs

## Limitations

1. does not write dialogue or prose
2. does not directly modify character psychology
3. does not invent new worldbuilding without approval
4. produces structured narrative plans, not final literary text
5. must respect character boundaries defined by Character Therapist
